T-Shaped Designers

An introduction to t-shaped design

By Emily Williams

Introduction

T-shaped skills or a t-shaped person was first officially referenced in 1991 by David Guest. With the theory and view really gaining momentum when it was endorsed by Tim Brown the CEO of IDEO Design Consultancy firm. The t-shaped is a term used to describe a person or professional with a wide and diverse set of skills.

What does a T-Shaped Designer have?

  1. A Range of Skills

    A deep understanding of one particular field, as well as a more general understanding of other fields.

  2. Empathetic Problem Solver

    Ability to see problems and potential solutions from multiple perspectives.

  3. Good Communicator

    Effectively communicate with people from different fields, and be able to explain their ideas to them.

The best way to become a t-shaped designer, is to concentrate on developing an expert skill, and a secondary skill. From here you should aim to maintain good relationships with cross-team members putting you in a good space to start growing further skills as part of your wider skillset!
